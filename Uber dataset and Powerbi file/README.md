1. Project Title :
Uber Operations & Performance Analytics Dashboard

2. Project Description
This interactive Power BI dashboard is designed to monitor and analyze Uber’s ride-sharing operations. It aggregates critical business metrics—such as booking volume, revenue (Booking Value), and service quality—into a single view. By visualizing data across different vehicle categories and timeframes, the dashboard allows management to identify peak performance periods, track fleet efficiency, and pinpoint areas where revenue is being lost due to failed bookings.

3. Problem Statement
Managing a large-scale ride-sharing service requires balancing driver supply with rider demand. The business currently faces the following challenges:

Revenue Leakage: Difficulty in quantifying "Lost Bookings" and understanding their impact on total potential revenue.

Fleet Optimization: Lack of clarity on which vehicle types (e.g., Sedan, Intercity, Comfort) are most profitable or most in demand.

Quality Control: The need to monitor both driver and customer satisfaction levels (ratings) to ensure long-term platform health.

Trend Tracking: Difficulty in visualizing how demand fluctuates over quarters to plan for seasonal scaling.

4. Step-by-Step Guide for Beginners
If you are opening this dashboard for the first time, follow these steps to understand the "story" the data is telling:

Step 1: Check the "Big Picture" (KPI Cards)
Start by looking at the large numbers (Cards).

Completed vs. Lost Bookings: See how many rides were successful compared to those that failed. A high "Lost Booking" count is a red flag for supply issues.

Booking Value: This is your total revenue. Check this first to see the financial health of the period.

Step 2: Understand Distance & Efficiency
Look at the Total Distance and Average Distance cards.

Amateur Tip: If the average distance is very short but booking counts are high, you have a high-frequency, short-trip market. If distances are long, your revenue per trip is likely higher.

Step 3: Analyze Trends Over Time
Look at the Area Chart and Clustered Column Chart.

These show Booking Count and Booking Value by Quarter.

What to look for: Are the lines and bars going up? If revenue is going up but booking counts are flat, you might be seeing more expensive, long-distance trips.

Step 4: Compare Vehicle Performance
Find the Bar Chart titled (or showing) Booking Value by Vehicle Type.

This tells you which car category is your "Cash Cow." You can see instantly if "Sedans" are outperforming "Intercity" rides.

Step 5: Monitor Service Quality
Look for the Customer Rating and Driver Rating cards (usually shown with star icons).

High ratings mean a healthy ecosystem. If customer ratings are low but driver ratings are high, there might be an issue with the app or pricing, rather than the drivers themselves.

Step 6: Interact and Filter
The dashboard is not just a static image.

Click an Image: Click on a vehicle type icon (like the Sedan image). The entire dashboard will update to show data only for that vehicle type.

Use the Date Slicer: Adjust the date slider to see how performance changed during a specific month or week.
